Must try food in the Philippines: Best dishes of Filipino food

Fancy a Filipino breakfast or a popular Filipino vegetable dish?

The Philippines is one of the most diverse and vibrant countries in Asia, but it's also one of the most underrated. Aside from its beautiful beaches, sun-kissed islands, and stunning scenery: there are so many great things about typical traditional Filipino food that you'll want to try! Filipino cuisine isn't only coconut milk and rice cakes, the country has a wide array of tastes and flavors to offer. Whether you're traveling there for business or pleasure, here are some must-try dishes that every foodie should try when visiting this beautiful country.

Sinigang

Sinigang is a famous Filipino dish made from meat, seafood and vegetables cooked in an acidic broth. It's a popular dish that's easy to make and full of vitamins. This popular Filipino stew can be made with many different sour fruits and leaves as the souring agent, but is mostly known for its use of tamarind.

Adobo

Adobo is a classic Filipino dish often made by braising meat or poultry in vinegar and garlic. It's one of the most popular dishes in the Philippines, and it's commonly served as a side dish with rice.

Adobo has been around for hundreds of years, but its exact origins remain largely unknown. The word "adobo" itself doesn't mean much; it comes from the Spanish word for "braise." However, many historians believe that this cooking technique originated with Chinese immigrants who settled on islands like Mindanao during trade routes between Asia and Europe centuries ago.

Kare-kare

This dish is a kind of stew made with oxtail, tripe and sometimes liver, which is cooked in peanut sauce. The meat and vegetables are cooked in soy sauce and pig’s blood and then served with bagoong (shrimp paste) on the side. It's usually served with white rice on the side as well.

Lumpia

Lumpia is a type of spring roll, which is a popular food you will find in many restaurants in the Philippines. It's a delicious meal that can be eaten any time throughout the day, whether you're on the go or at home with family and friends.

Lumpia is healthy too, since it's made with shredded carrots and cabbage, along with other vegetables and spices such as garlic, ginger and soy sauce. The best part? Lumpia comes in many different flavors—from red chili to vegetarian options!

Pancit canton

Pancit canton is a popular noodle dish made with rice noodles, pork and chicken. It is a popular street food in the Philippines and is often served for breakfast or lunch. If your are coming to the Philippines you will surely see this dish many times along your travels.

Lechon

Lechon is another Filipino food made of roasted pig. It can be served as the main dish or in a sandwich. Lechon originated from Spain and was introduced to the Philippines by Spanish colonizers. Today, lechon is considered one of the national dishes in the Philippines and it’s very popular especially during special occasions such as Christmas and New Year’s Eve. This traditional dish has many variations depending on where you eat it but it usually includes pork belly or skin that's been seasoned with salt, pepper, garlic and other spices before being cooked over an open fire pit until tender enough to fall off the bone!

Lugaw

This filipino comfort food is a fairly easy one to make. What is essentially rice porridge and made with simple ingredients like rice, ginger, garlic bits, and egg. A variant, including chicken and a more sticky rice is known as "Arroz Caldo", but is a bit more work to make.

Food in the Philippines: Conclusion

We hope that by reading through our list of must try food in the Philippines, you were able to find some dishes that you didn’t know about before. It’s such an exciting country with so many different flavors and styles of cuisine (even if you lived in the Philippines, you might not have tried all the traditional Filipino dishes, which are often often regional and can vary greatly), so why not try something new? Make sure to try out some local Filipino restaurants when visiting and be sure to visit Manila, capital of the Philippines, to find out the great variety of the food culture this beautiful country has to offer!
